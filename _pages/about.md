

I am a third-year PhD candidate in [Biomedical Engineering](https://bme.ucdavis.edu/) at the University of California, Davis, advised by [Dr. Xiaomo Chen](https://www.xiaomolab-ucdavis.com/) in the Cognitive Control Lab. I am also a proud **NeuralStorm Fellow**. Prior to UC Davis, I completed my MS in Bioengineering at the University of Illinois Chicago, and worked as a Research Specialist at the [Center for Sleep and Consciousness](https://centerforsleepandconsciousness.med.wisc.edu/) at UW-Madison under Dr. Melanie Boly and Dr. Aaron Struck, where I investigated sleep as a biomarker for epilepsy using intracranial and high-density EEG.

## Research

My PhD research sits at the intersection of systems neuroscience and neuroengineering. I study the dynamics of neural circuits and their responses to perturbation using electrical microstimulation.

## Background

Before focusing on cortical stimulation, I worked extensively with clinical neurological patient data — applying signal processing pipelines to intracranial EEG and high-density EEG to study sleep-wake dynamics in epilepsy patients. This clinical exposure across UC Davis, UW-Madison, and the UC system continues to inform how I think about translational applications of neural stimulation.

My long-term goal is to bridge the gap between fundamental neuroscience and applied neuroengineering — developing stimulation methods and protocols that can translate from the lab into therapeutic and cognitive neurotech applications.
